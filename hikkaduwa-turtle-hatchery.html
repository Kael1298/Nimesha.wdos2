Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, also known as the Hikkaduwa Turtle Conservation Sanctuary, is a well-known turtle conservation project located in Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka. The hatchery aims to protect sea turtles, particularly the endangered species, by providing a safe environment for their eggs to hatch and releasing the hatchlings into the ocean. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery actively collects sea turtle eggs from vulnerable nests along the coastal areas, safeguarding them from various threats and predators. Once the eggs hatch, the hatchlings are nurtured in tanks until they are strong and ready for release. Visitors to the hatchery can observe these adorable baby turtles and learn about the conservation efforts in place to protect these magnificent creatures.

In addition to conservation efforts, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery also focuses on public awareness and education about the importance of sea turtle conservation. They conduct informative sessions and engage with visitors to raise awareness about the challenges faced by sea turtles and the actions required to protect them. It's important to note thzat while turtle hatcheries provide valuable support for sea turtle conservation, it's equally crucial to promote responsible tourism practices and sustainable efforts to preserve the natural habitats of sea turtles. Visitors to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery can contribute to these efforts by adhering to guidelines provided by the hatchery and supporting their initiatives. Please note that the availability of specific activities, programs, or the status of the hatchery may vary, and it's advisable to check with the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery directly or reliable sources for the most up-to-date information before planning a visit.
